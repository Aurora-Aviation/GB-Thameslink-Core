# Thameslink Core

## Network

The central Thameslink Core tunnel (the Snow Hill tunnel) was built in January 1866, and the modern Thameslink cross-city service becan service in 1988, with Class 319s. In 2017, they were retired in favour of the 8 and 12 coach Class 700 fleet built by Desiro, featuring ATO, ETCS/AWS dual functionality and Third Rail and OHLE capabilities. These signalling capabilities now allow the Thameslink Core to run at a maximum of 24 trains per hour in each direction.
Although the technology is very advanced in this section of track, the tunnes were originally created using the cut and cover method, and the tunnel is around 20-30 metres deep.
Blackfriars features two terminating platforms, used both by Thameslink and Southeastern.

## Simulation

## Operational Tips

- Headcodes consist of one digit to signify the service type (in this simulation only 1,2,3,5 and 9 appear), a letter depending on the route, and a double digit service number that increases with later services.
- General Thameslink services headcode letters:
    - T: Bedford-Brighton Route
    - R: Bedford-Three Bridges Route
    - S: Cambridge-Brighton Route
    - O: St Albans City-Sutton Route
    - V: St Albans City-Sutton Route
    - L: To/From East Grinstead
    - Y: To/From Sevenoaks
    - K: To/From Orpington (apart from 9K13 and 9K17)
    - Z: To/From Blackfriars P1 or P2 (non-terminating platforms)
- Southeastern service headcode letters:
    - M: Blackfriars-Beckenham Junction Route
    - Y: Crayford-Blackfriars Route
    - F: Maidstone East-Blackfriars Route
    - K: Blackfriars-Rochester or Gillingham (Kent)-Blackfriars
- Elephant & Castle Platform 2 is bidirectional, however isn't normally used under normal operating circumstances for services towards Blackfriars. It can be used if a train is delayed at Platform 1, or if a points up ahead (e.g. one to change from the Platform 1 E&C Line to the line for Blackfriars Platform 3) has failed.
- Smithfield C.H.S, St. Pancras Intl. Service Siding, and STP High Level do not have any timetabled services.
- Some services turn around at Blackfriars Platform 1.
- Some Southbound services enter from T/F West Hampstead TL on the Northbound track, as they need to get to Platform 3 or 4.
- If a significant delay of a train in the core occurs at a station, the crossovers may be used to route trains around the delayed train.
